Madrid is one of the world's padel epicenters. Spain's capital is home to some of Europe's largest padel complexes and a player community that grows every year. Whether you're a local or visiting, these are the best options for playing padel in Madrid.

## Best Padel Courts in Madrid

### [Ciudad de la Raqueta](/torneos/718a60ec-bae4-4d36-90f5-cf9099d6812c)

**C. del Monasterio de El Paular 2, 28049 Madrid**

One of Europe's largest padel and tennis complexes. 22 padel courts (10 indoor, 12 outdoor) and a stadium seating 800. Open to the public, not just members. Features a restaurant, café, summer terrace, and parking. Hours: Monday to Sunday 8am to midnight.

**Contact**: +34 917 297 922 · ciudadraqueta@ciudadraqueta.com

### [Madrid Central Padel](/torneos/33127efc-2b34-47b2-abaa-d8be027b3e29)

**C/ Boyer 28, 28052 Madrid (Vicálvaro)**

17 padel courts: 14 indoor climate-controlled (2 with official World Padel Tour grass) and 3 outdoor. Highlights include the Central Paquito Navarro stadium. Also features a pickleball court, 2 Padbol courts, a padel school, specialty store, panoramic café, and large parking area. Hours: Mon-Fri 9am-midnight, Sat-Sun 9am-10:30pm.

**Contact**: +34 910 514 100 · recepcion@madridcentralpadel.com

## Playing Padel in Madrid: What You Need to Know

- **Booking**: Most clubs in Madrid allow online booking via Playtomic, their own website, or by phone. Book 1-2 days in advance, especially for evening slots.
- **Prices**: Courts in Madrid range from 8€ to 20€ per person/hour depending on the time slot and court type (indoor tends to be more expensive).
- **Seasons**: Unlike Buenos Aires, Madrid has distinct seasons. In summer (June-September), outdoor courts are ideal for evening play. In winter, climate-controlled indoor courts are a must.
- **Community**: Madrid has a massive padel community. Apps like Playtomic or local WhatsApp groups are a great way to find playing partners.
